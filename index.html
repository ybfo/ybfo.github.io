About me

I am a MIT Micromaster student in the Pengcheng Lab at Shenzhen, advised by Prof. Xiongyu Hu. My research interests include Application of Artificial Intelligence in Civil Engineering, Generative AI, and 3D reconstruction and Solving practical engineering problems through mathematical modeling. I have published over 10 papers and received over 20 awards, including 8 national awards. I was formerly the academic director of the Mathematical Modeling Association at Southwest Jiaotong University. I appreciate simple and elegant solutions.

Before this, I received my B.S. from Southwest Jiaotong University in 2025.6.

Research

I used to focus on image and video steganography and structural engineering automation. Now my main work is:

  • Visual applications of generative AI: including 3D reconstruction, video prediction, domain generalization and adaptation, etc.
  • Tunnel artificial intelligence and automation: including self-driving, parameter prediction, agent establishment and monitoring.
  • I also have considerable research interests in embodied intelligence and large models.
  • I believe that the use of physical information and high-dimensional spatial information will significantly affect the design of existing models, so I also have certain research inputs into related physical information models and mathematical theories.
